Childhood

My love for video games began very early, even before I could afford any console or pc. Nintendo and Playstation played a role in my passion for video games as they were the first consoles I ever owned. Well, not owned because we didn't have any money for video games or any console for that matter. What I have had was the chance to borrow consoles and games from friends who were fortunate enough to buy them.

I still remember Power Rangers, Mario, Ratchet and Clank, Resident Evil, and so on from hours of playing these games. One game stood out for me, God of War and yes, I know... I was too young for such a game but I played it anyway.

It all changed a bit during adolescence because during this time of my life, I moved from Brazil to Germany.

Adolescence

Upon arriving in Germany with my mother and sorting out our lives, eventually, I owned a PC and Playstation. Then my passion for gaming continued, but this time I wanted to do more than just play games.

Throughout my adolescence and highschool period, I had begun to modify games, for example like changing car models, character skins, creating new textures for the assets, and so on. It was all fascinating, but after finishing highschool, I sadly made the wrong choice of finding full-time jobs instead of going to college to continue and further improve my education.

After four and a half years of working full-time, I had reached my lowest. A very depressing time in my life, full of unhappiness and this is what pushed me into quitting my job to go back to college at 24 years old and thankfully this is a very normal thing for young adults to do in Germany which gave me comfort in knowing I wouldn't be alone or wasting my time.

Adulthood

As a student, I now focus on my passion that I sadly had putten away due to work and started learning game development after college hours. Anytime I'm free, I invest time into learning.

My first game project was a flappy bird type of game called "Grumpy" inspired by youtube tutorials. The goal of "Grumpy" was to get familiar with a game engine such as Unity. My second game "Savior" was a survival game, to teach me how to program a simple AI, character controller , and a little bit of character art. Then I moved on to a more complex project "Fatebringer" based on a shooter game that I still play to this day. It taught me to program more complex aspects of 2D game design.

After finishing three games in 2D, I then decided to move on to 3D projects, thus creating my very first 3D game. Another challenge to overcome and after six months of learning and developing, I created and published my fourth game called "Zeo".

These four games were all done and published within one year of learning game development, and now I can create my own assets, program whatever is needed for my games, and all of it was achieved by loving what I do.

Passion and dedication are two very powerful things. My goal is to become a professional game developer.
